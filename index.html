    Sound Barrier

    Won 2nd place in the Fall 2020 TAGD Semester Game Jam: "Something's Missing"
    September 2020 - November 2020


    A first person stealth/puzzle shooter in which the walls around you must be shot to be rendered visible; however, your gun is loud, and will alert enemies in the area.

    Development Summary

    What I worked on
    • Finite State Machine AI for stealth-based enemies
    • Shader Graph effects, simple post-processing
    • Optimizing large amounts of visual effects on screen at once
    What went well
    • Workflow was intuitive- the base game was fully made with just 3 levels before I began to make more.
      • This meant that I could understand the limits of my code and design before creating a challenging set of levels, to avoid overscoping.
    • More design-oriented thought process: Less coding and more planning into each system to ensure less errors down the line.
    • Worked to my advantages- my lack of experience in 3D art allowed me to work with that and keep a simplistic style.
    What didn't go as well, lessons learned
    • Some systems weren't quite as solid/consistent as they could have been - AI in particular had a couple of reworks.
      • A major roadblock was attempting to integrate sprinting into the list of things that could alert enemies to you. This ultimately made the game too difficult, however, and was scrapped.
    • Could have had a much more varied field of playtesters to get more perspectives on how the game was playing.

    Alchemancer

    Entry in the Fall 2019 TAGD Semester Game Jam: "Chain Reaction"
    September 2019 - November 2019



    An infinite 2D action game focused on chaining together exploding potions in a mysterious dungeon.

    Development Summary

    What I worked on
    • Simple chemistry system design
      • Potion reactions between enemies and player-placed potions
    • File parsing system for maximum development efficiency
      • Potions and their crafting recipes were entered into a CSV.
      • This way all we had to do is program the effects in.
    • First game ever seen to completion.
    What went well
    • Learnt about the structure of Unity and C#, as well as the basics of threading and vector spaces.
    • Team dynamic was helpful, with frequent idea sessions to improve the backend systems of our game.
    • Managed to fit in some stretch goals, such as a level editor with online sharing capabilities.
    What didn't go as well, lessons learned
    • Code was a little messy sometimes, likely because of how new the team was to Unity/C#.
    • Had some issues getting on the same page in terms of design- better communication would have fixed that.
    • Dove into coding way too quickly, with not much thought towards properly planning the product out.

    Burger Truckers

    Entry for the Gamemaker's Toolkit 2020 Game Jam: "Out of Control"


    Development Summary

    What I worked on
    • Cooking system game logic, along with a request ticket system that works on an infinite difficulty progression.
    • Optimization of assets for the sake of lag reduction
    • Cooperating with an artist on a severe time crunch
    What went well
    • Game logic came out really nice, despite some (now cleaned up) spaghetti code.
    • Having a completed product for my first 48 hour game jam.
    • Spent a nontrivial amount of time on design, incorporating the theme into the game loop.
      • The player's goal is to maintain control of the truck and the orders.
    What didn't go as well, lessons learned
    • Even with a simple gameplay loop, likely overscoped.
      • Should try to do 2D for 48 hour jams in future.
      • Request system was a little too complex- had much less time to debug than needed.
    • Could have established better workflow with artists to prevent any panics.
    • Thirty more minutes of planning could have saved hours of bug fixing.
